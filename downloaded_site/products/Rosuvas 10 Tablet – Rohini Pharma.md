About Rosuvas 10 Tablet 

Rosuvas 10 Tablet helps reduce bad cholesterol (low-density lipoprotein, LDL) and triglyceride levels, thereby reducing the risk of heart attack and stroke in the future. When the blood’s cholesterol levels are out of balance (unhealthy), it can lead to high cholesterol, sometimes referred to as hyperlipidemia or dyslipidemia. High blood cholesterol causes your arteries to get smaller and become blocked, which ultimately leads to cardiac conditions like heart attacks and strokes. 

Rosuvas 10 Tablet contains rosuvastatin, which works by blocking liver enzymes, causing the liver to make less cholesterol. Thus, Rosuvas 10 Tablet lowers the level of cholesterol and triglycerides in the blood when lifestyle changes (like a low-fat diet) on their own have failed. It also improves your body’s ability to remove bad cholesterol from your blood. 

Not everyone who is taking Rosuvas 10 Tablet will get these side effects. The possible side effects of Rosuvas 10 Tablet may include myalgia (muscle pain), asthenia (fatigue), nausea, headache, abdominal pain, muscle weakness, and daytime drowsiness. Most of these side effects of Rosuvas 10 Tablet do not require medical attention and gradually resolve over time. However, if the side effects are persistent, reach out to your doctor. 

Take Rosuvas 10 Tablet as advised. Your physician will decide the dosage based on your medical condition. You can take Rosuvas 10 Tablet with or without food. It is advised not to take more than the recommended dose of Rosuvas 10 Tablet . Do not chew, crush, or break the medicine. It is crucial to have your lipid profile checked regularly. 

Inform your physician if you are allergic to Rosuvas 10 Tablet . Before starting the dose of Rosuvas 10 Tablet , tell your doctor if you have kidney problems, are pregnant or breastfeeding, have diabetes, severe lung disease, or have musculoskeletal disorders (like myopathy and rhabdomyolysis). You should avoid taking Rosuvas 10 Tablet with immune system affecting medicine (cyclosporine) or anti-HIV medicines (lopinavir, ritonavir, or atazanavir) as it might have a serious effect. 

Rosuvas 10 Tablet is only one part of a comprehensive treatment program that should include a healthy, low-fat diet, regular exercise, and weight reduction. You can normally eat while taking this medicine, but avoid high-fat foods. It is advised to avoid smoking and alcohol intake, as they can lead to other side effects.

Uses of Rosuvas 10 Tablet 

Rosuvas 10 Tablet is used in the treatment of hyperlipidemia (high cholesterol), prevention of heart attack, and prevention of stroke. The detailed uses of Rosuvas 10 Tablet are as follows: 

- Cholesterol Management: Rosuvas 10 Tablet is primarily used to lower LDL cholesterol (commonly known as ‘bad’ cholesterol) and triglycerides in the blood, reducing the risk of heart disease. - Cardiovascular Risk Reduction: By lowering cholesterol levels, Rosuvas 10 Tablet significantly decreases the chances of heart attacks and strokes in individuals with cardiovascular conditions or risk factors. - Familial Hypercholesterolemia Treatment: Rosuvas 10 Tablet is prescribed to manage lipid levels in patients with familial hypercholesterolemia, a genetic disorder leading to very high cholesterol levels. - Improving HDL Cholesterol: Rosuvas 10 Tablet also boosts HDL cholesterol (‘good’ cholesterol), promoting better heart health. - Preventing Atherosclerosis: By maintaining healthy cholesterol levels, Rosuvas 10 Tablet helps prevent plaque build-up in arteries, supporting overall vascular health and circulation. 

Directions for Use 

- Rosuvas 10 Tablet can be taken with or without food. - It is recommended to take Rosuvas 10 Tablet once daily or as advised by your doctor. - Swallow Rosuvas 10 Tablet as a whole with a glass of water. - If you have trouble swallowing the medicine, open it and sprinkle the contents over a small amount (tablespoon) of soft food (curd, honey, tomato ketchup, vanilla or chocolate flavour pudding) and consume it. - Do not crush or chew this medication. 

Medicinal Benefits 

- Rosuvas 10 Tablet lowers the risk of cardiovascular disease and coronary events like heart attack, stroke, and heart-related chest pain (angina). Rosuvas 10 Tablet is beneficial in patients who are at high risk of these events. - Rosuvas 10 Tablet plays a vital role in slowing atherosclerosis progress (blocked artery walls of the heart due to fat/lipid deposition leading to heart attack and stroke) in adult patients. - Rosuvas 10 Tablet reduces the ‘bad’ cholesterol and increases the ‘good’ cholesterol by blocking our body’s production of ‘bad’ cholesterol. Thus, improves your body’s ability to remove bad cholesterol from your blood. - It is also prescribed in children (aged eight years or more) suffering from familial hypercholesterolemia (a genetic disease resulting in high cholesterol) along with lipid-lowering pills and other dietary measures. 

How Rosuvas 10 Tablet Works 
Rosuvas 10 Tablet contains Rosuvastatin. It works by blocking liver enzymes, causing the liver to produce less cholesterol. Rosuvas 10 Tablet also increases the liver’s uptake and breakdown of cholesterol already in the blood. Thus, Rosuvas 10 Tablet lowers the levels of lipids known as cholesterol and triglycerides in the blood when lifestyle changes (like a low-fat diet) have failed. Rosuvas 10 Tablet reduces bad cholesterol and increases good cholesterol by blocking our body’s production of bad cholesterol. It also improves the body’s ability to remove it from the blood.

What if I have taken an overdose of Rosuvas 10 Tablet 
Do not take more than the prescribed dose of Rosuvas 10 Tablet as it may cause overdose. If you suspect you have taken overdose, please consult a doctor immediately. 
Drug Warnings 

- Avoid taking Rosuvas 10 Tablet if you are allergic to it. - Patients with proteinuria (protein in urine) or haematuria (blood in urine) might require dose adjustment. - It is recommended that you have a liver enzyme test regularly if you are regularly taking Rosuvas 10 Tablet . - A prolonged intake of Rosuvas 10 Tablet may cause muscular disorders like myopathy and rhabdomyolysis with kidney damage. - Inform your doctor if you are an alcoholic, as excess alcohol intake may cause serious side effects. - Caution should be exercised if you are taking any blood thinners or anticoagulants, such as warfarin or coumarin. - A 2-hour gap should be maintained between the intake of Rosuvas 10 Tablet and antacids, such as magnesium hydroxide and aluminium hydroxide. - Increases in HbA1c and fasting glucose levels can be observed in individuals who regularly take Rosuvas 10 Tablet . Therefore, you should inform the doctor before undergoing these diabetes tests. - Rosuvas 10 Tablet is safe for use in children above 8 years of age suffering from familial hypercholesterolemia (a genetic disease resulting in high cholesterol), along with lipid-lowering pills and other dietary measures. Your paediatrician will decide the dose of the medicine based on your child’s age and body weight. - It is always important to check the expiry of the medicine.
